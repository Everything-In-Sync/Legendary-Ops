Do I Pay in Advance and what is your Cancellation Policy?

Yes, all orders require a 50% non-refundable deposit in order to reserve your event date (see policy on weather cancellations below). The remaining balance is due at least 48 hours prior to your party. In the event of a cancellation, if cancelled at least 48 hours prior to your event, you will receive a refund of any monies paid minus the deposit. If cancelled less than 48 hours from the event, no refund will be given. However, you will have a raincheck to use for up to one year from the original date of your event (we're awesome like that). If you do not use your raincheck within that year, you forfeit all monies sent to Legendary Ops, LLC.

What is your Weather Policy?

We understand the stress that our customers go through when their loved one’s party has to be canceled due to weather. We don't like anyone to miss out on a Legendary Ops LLC game experience, which is why we will apply your deposit to another date in the future - up to a year to use from the original event date (how cool are we?!).

We can work with you to make this determination no more than 48 hours before the event. This allows our customers enough time to notify guests. On the day of the event and during periods of severe weather (rain, high winds, etc.), we reserve the right to cancel your reservation. If conditions are not too severe, we will give you the option of keeping the event or accepting a rain check for a future date.

If you decide to keep the term of the event agreement (regardless of the weather) there will be no refunds. There will be no refunds within 3 hours of the party start time. The wind speed will dictate to us the equipment we will be allowed to use - for example, some obstacles and tents cannot be set up in wind conditions of 15 miles per hour or more.

Are we Responsible for Damage in any way?

Yes and No

You are not responsible for normal wear and tear of our equipment. For example, obstacles may develop wear in areas over a period of time. If this happens, please alert us at once so we can remedy the situation. If, however, damage occurs due to failure to follow our safety rules or negligence (for example, jumping on the obstacles or deliberately getting them wet, etc.), you will be responsible for all damages up to and including replacement of the equipment (which can cost thousands of dollars).

We don't want you (or us) to be in that situation, which is why we have you sign and initial all of our safety rules. Negligence or damage to equipment that occurs while not following the operating instructions provided at the time of the event is your responsibility.

How many People do we need to Play?

We need at least 2 players to run a game.
If you have just a 2-person group and there isn't anyone else playing, you are more than welcome to play 1 vs. 1 in grudge matches.
If you have a larger group and would like to play with your group only, we recommend coming in earlier in the day when we are generally not as busy.
However, unless you buy out the entire round, we cannot guarantee that you will not have others added to your group.



How are Teams Determined?

Our computer will automatically select teams based on XP to make them as balanced as possible. You may ask the game master to change your team but please be respectful if they choose not to.
Normally they are more than happy to keep friends together but sometimes it just isn't possible.



Will there be other people added to my group's game?

Yes, unless you buy out the round, there may be players from outside your group added to your round.



Can Kids Play?

Yes. Players must be 8 years or older and at least 48 inches tall. Please note that our general play event arena is designed for older children and adults.
The lights, music, sounds and intense gameplay may be overwhelming to some children. For their own safety, children under the age of 8 may not enter the arena at any time.
Please note that kid-specific and/or private parties are age-appropriate.
This is part of our party setup communication with the client.



Do I Have to sign a Waiver if my Child Plays, but I don't?

Yes, a child under 18 cannot sign the waiver legally so a parent or guardian must fill out a waiver for themselves plus the child.



How long does it take to get set up the first time?

The fastest way to be ready for your session is to already have your waiver signed and a Callsign created. The waiver is a must have, but if you choose to create a Callsign at our HQ (event lobby) it might take a few minutes.
Once you've created your Callsign and signed the waiver you will never have to worry about them again (except for the waiver renewal which only happens after a year of signing it).

Public vs. Private – What are the differences?

With a private Event you have control over the arena, location and game. Game types (we will provide guidance), loadouts and music as well as who is on each team and team colors.
Each private party has a “Person of Honor” that either you, the host, selects or we can select. That person will receive a free Legendary Ops t-shirt.



How many people can play at one time?

We have the ability to support 20 players at one time depending on venue size. Please contact us to discuss your venue and schedule a site survey.



Where can you set up a private event?

We are self-contained with our own power, Internet and WiFi. This means we can do parks, common areas, convention space as well as your backyard.
We do not coordinate customer/host access, permits or reservations, so please check with your venue and local authorities before booking us for a location.



Do you provide obstacles?

Yes. That is part of our service. We do not charge for the number of obstacles. We will setup a custom arena optimized for game play taking into consideration the size provided.



Do you provide food?

No.



Do you provide permits and reservations?

No. We do not coordinate customer/host access, permits or reservations. Please check with your venue and local authorities to determine the requirements before booking us for a location.



Is setup/teardown part of the Event time?

No. We arrive early to set up so you get the entire time to play, and when your time has concluded, we will then take everything down and leave.
For example, if your event is two hours, that is two hours for game time. Game time is affected by player availability, briefing, stamina, etc., however we are available to run games for the full block of time.
